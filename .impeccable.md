## Design Context

### Users
This project is for builders and researchers who want to validate a webcam-based lip-reading prototype in a browser. The primary job is to capture short live camera segments, push them into a Chinese visual speech recognition backend, and quickly judge whether the pipeline is usable.

### Brand Personality
Precise, technical, calm. The interface should feel like a research console rather than a consumer gadget or a flashy AI landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm editorial lab console. Use paper-tinted neutrals, oxidized red accents, dark ink text, strong data-panel framing, and a clear sense of system state. Favor a light interface with deliberate contrast and visible instrumentation.

### Design Principles
- Put operational state first: camera, backend, queue, and inference readiness should always be obvious.
- Treat uncertainty as a product feature: show latency, model status, and setup blockers instead of hiding them.
- Keep the live capture stage dominant so users can position their face without hunting for the preview.
- Use plain, technical language over marketing copy.
- Make the prototype useful even before the heavy model is fully installed by surfacing concrete setup steps in-product.
